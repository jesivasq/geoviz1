Educational Attainment in the U.S. by Percentage of State Population

Highest level of education achieved

Source: USDA Economic Research Service, Educational Attainment for the U.S. (https://www.ers.usda.gov/data-products/county-level-data-sets/county-level-data-sets-download-data/); background map source: modified from Visualizing Data by Ben Fry (http://benfry.com/writing/archives/3/)

The map above shows the highest level of education achieved by adults (age 25 and over) in a given state during the census years 1970, 1980, 1990, or 2000, and the American Community Survey average for the period of 2012-2016.

Take as a baseline the percentage of adults who did not complete high school. If you compare the numbers from 1970 to the 2012-2016 numbers, there is a distinct decrease. The national average has gone from 48% not completing high school in 1970 to only 13% percent in the most recent surveys. That means 87% of the current adult population has at least a high school education.

In addition, 29.1% of adults have attended some college and 30.3% have at least a bachelor’s degree, meaning that almost 60% of the current U.S. adult population has attended college (see figure below).
